ABOUT ME

I'm originally from Cortlandt Manor New York and currently reside in Houston, Texas.

In high school I was an avid runner and was excited to spread my passion for the sport. I placed 6th nationally in the AAU Junior Olympics my junior year of high school and went on to place 1st in the the New York State Public High School Track & Field Championships.

Later in high school I founded the Stay On Track Nonprofit Organization, establishing an annual track and field meet for children that brought together hundreds of participants and volunteers each year. I passed on the leadership of the organization to the younger members of my track team as my attention shifted to college and my future plans. A couple years later, I'm a NCAA Division 1 Track athlete at Rice University and am doing as well as ever.

By high school, I founded the Stay On Track Nonprofit Organization, establishing an annual track and field meet for children that perenial brought together hundreds of participants and volunteers. The company was eventually acquired as my interest in gaming waned (along with inflated revenues of the dot-com boom), but a passion for online business & design remained. Twenty years, countless projects, and numerous ups-and-downs later, I'm still as obsessed as ever.

Prior to Squarespace, I was an early employee at a Seattle-based education startup, where I built the flagship consumer website and grew it into a top-500 property (Alexa, 2007–2010). This helped the company become one of the most profitable (net margin) and fastest-growing private companies in the US, leading to a $160M acquisition.

I currently reside in Houston and am a member of Wiess College of Rice University, where I serve as Internal Vice President and Webmaster. I've founded and head a number of committees, where I aspire to ignite passion for initiative and originality. You can visit my work on the college website at teamwiess.com; it allows me to impart my life's positive influences onto a new generation, which is incredibly rewarding.
