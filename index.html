ABOUT ME

I'm originally from Cortlandt Manor New York and currently reside in Houston, Texas.

I've spent a lot of my time consumed in maniacical frenzies. I've always enjoyed picking up something new off the ground, and —literally— running with it.

By high school I had gone quite far in my knack for running, a sport I had unintentionally collided with. I placed 6th nationally in cross country at the USATF Junior Olympic Games and first in the mile at the New York State Public High School Championships. I thought it might be of use to a launch a means for other adolescents to collide into a sport that too would go on to be a great positive influence on their lives.

I launched the Stay On Track Foundation, a 501(c)3 nonprofit organization that sought to introduce the fun and competitive facets of athleticism to children in grade school. We brought together nearly a hundred participants and volunteers for a child-friendly track meet that we called the Mini Olympics.

My knack for running followed me to college at Rice University, where I competed on the NCAA track team. You can peak at my work for their website here.

Rice is where I discovered my interest for data, design, and development, and where it came to balloon into an untetherable passion.

I currently reside in Houston and am a member of Wiess College of Rice University, where I serve as Internal Vice President and Webmaster. You can visit my work on the college website here.

I will be working as a Technology Analyst for JP Morgan and Co. in Houston this summer and plan to continue my work as a freelance developer.
